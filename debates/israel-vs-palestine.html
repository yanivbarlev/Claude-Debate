All 10 Questions — Full Debate
Q1

Who has the stronger historical and legal claim to the land between the Jordan River and the Mediterranean Sea?

Round 1 · Pro-Israel

Israel holds the stronger historical and legal claim based on a convergence of ancient history, modern legal instruments, and international recognition. The Jewish people maintained an unbroken presence for over 3,000 years. The 1917 Balfour Declaration, 1920 San Remo Conference, 1922 League of Nations Mandate, and UN Partition Plan of 1947 (Resolution 181) all affirmed the legitimacy of a Jewish state. Israel was admitted to the UN in 1949 and recognized by over 160 nations. No equivalent international legal architecture exists for any rival claim to the entire territory.

Round 1 · Pro-Palestine

Palestinians hold the stronger claim under historical continuity and modern international law. Arab Palestinians constituted roughly 90% of the population in Ottoman Palestine as late as 1878. Resolution 181 allocated 56% of territory to a Jewish state despite Palestinian majority. UNSC Resolutions 242 and 338 affirm acquisition of land by force is inadmissible. The ICJ's 2004 opinion confirmed the occupation violates international humanitarian law. The Balfour Declaration was a colonial-era promise by a government with no sovereign right to gift Palestinian land. Biblical claims cannot override modern property rights and the post-1945 legal order.

Round 2 · Pro-Israel Rebuttal

The demographic argument is a red herring — Ottoman census data was unreliable, and "Palestine" had no distinct political identity. The 1947 Partition Plan was accepted by Jewish leadership and rejected by Arab states who then launched a war. The Nakba was a consequence of that Arab-initiated war, not a premeditated ethnic cleansing program. UNSC 242 calls for negotiated withdrawal, not unconditional withdrawal — the deliberate omission of "all" was intentional. The 1922 Mandate, 1947 UN partition, and 1948 declaration are the legal pillars, not biblical claims.

Round 2 · Pro-Palestine Rebuttal

Continuous Jewish presence does not equal exclusive sovereign claim — these are entirely distinct legal concepts. The Balfour Declaration was a colonial instrument that never consulted the 90% Arab majority. Resolution 181's legitimacy is undermined by the fact that the General Assembly lacks authority to partition territory against its inhabitants' will — this is settled UN Charter law. 160 nations recognizing Israel is irrelevant to whether Palestinian rights were violated — 160 nations also recognize Palestinian self-determination per repeated UNGA resolutions.

Q2

Is Israel's military occupation of the West Bank and blockade of Gaza justified on security grounds?

Round 1 · Pro-Israel

Yes — Israel withdrew every soldier and settler from Gaza in 2005. Hamas seized control in 2007 and launched thousands of rockets. The Second Intifada killed over 1,000 Israeli civilians. The security barrier reduced suicide attacks by over 90 percent. The October 7, 2023 attack killed 1,200 people and took 250 hostages from territory Israel had vacated. Under international humanitarian law, a state has the obligation to defend its population. Security measures in the West Bank exist precisely to prevent a repeat.

Round 1 · Pro-Palestine

The security justification collapses under empirical scrutiny. Nearly six decades of occupation with no resolution indicates the strategy has failed by its own metric. The Gaza blockade restricts food, medicine, and construction materials. UN documents warned Gaza would be "unlivable" by 2020 — accurate. Israeli documents from 2010 showed officials calculating minimum calories — collective punishment prohibited under Article 33 of the Fourth Geneva Convention. The occupation generates the very radicalization it purports to prevent. Ehud Barak acknowledged he too would have joined a militant organization were he Palestinian.

Round 2 · Pro-Israel Rebuttal

The "calorie calculation" refers to a 2008 document estimating subsistence thresholds during a temporary tightening, not a policy of deliberate starvation. The West Bank barrier reduced suicide bombings by over 90% after 2002's 130 Israeli civilian deaths in a single year. Gaza has zero Israeli settlers post-2005 yet produced October 7 — directly challenging the premise that occupation is the cause of violence. Barak's hypothetical statement is anecdotal speculation, not policy.

Round 2 · Pro-Palestine Rebuttal

Gaza was never free. The UN, ICRC, and virtually every international legal body classified Gaza as occupied territory even post-2005 because Israel controlled its airspace, coastline, borders, population registry, and electromagnetic sphere. Hamas's rise was partly enabled by Israel's deliberate policy of weakening the PLO. Israel's response to October 7 killed over 50,000 Palestinians — the majority civilians and children per UN and WHO data — destroyed 90% of housing, and induced famine. The ICJ ordered Israel to prevent genocide. Self-defense cannot legally justify collective punishment of 2.3 million people.

Q3

Does Israel meet the criteria of an apartheid state in its treatment of Palestinians?

Round 1 · Pro-Israel

Israel does not meet the legal definition. Arab citizens hold full voting rights, sit in the Knesset, serve as Supreme Court justices, and in 2021 the Ra'am Party joined a governing coalition. No apartheid state has ever integrated its "oppressed" group into the highest levels of government. The West Bank situation reflects a temporary, conflict-driven security arrangement — not a racial ideology. South Africa's apartheid was a permanent, codified racial system applied to citizens. Israel applies civilian law to citizens and military law to a non-citizen population in a contested zone. The apartheid charge is strategically deployed to delegitimize Israel entirely.

Round 1 · Pro-Palestine

By the legal definition in the 1973 International Convention and the Rome Statute, Israel's system qualifies. In the West Bank, Jewish settlers and Palestinian residents live under entirely separate legal systems on the same land — settlers under Israeli civil law, Palestinians under military law with a 99.7% conviction rate. This legal bifurcation on the basis of ethnicity is apartheid's definitional core. Human Rights Watch (2021), Amnesty International (2022), B'Tselem (2021), and the UN Special Rapporteur have reached this conclusion. Desmond Tutu stated the situation "reminded me of the apartheid regime." The Nation-State Law explicitly privileges Jewish identity.

Round 2 · Pro-Israel Rebuttal

The apartheid label is polemical, not legal. South African apartheid was racial segregation within a single sovereign state. Palestinian Arabs in the West Bank and Gaza are not Israeli citizens — they are stateless populations in disputed territories where final-status negotiations were never completed. Military courts exist because the civilian court system has no jurisdiction over non-citizen combatants. Even the ICJ's 2024 advisory opinion did not declare Israel an apartheid state.

Round 2 · Pro-Palestine Rebuttal

The "Arab citizens vote" defense was used to justify South African Bantustans — formal rights within a discriminatory system do not negate apartheid. Israel's own Nation-State Law (2018) explicitly downgrades Arabic and reserves national self-determination exclusively for Jewish people — codified ethnic hierarchy in Basic Law. The ICJ's 2024 advisory opinion declared the occupation itself illegal and called for its termination within 12 months. When millions in the West Bank live under military law while settlers on the same land live under civil law, that is legal segregation by ethnicity meeting the Rome Statute's intent requirement.

Q4

Are Palestinian armed resistance movements justified under international law as liberation struggles?

Round 1 · Pro-Israel

International law permits armed resistance against military targets — not deliberate mass murder of civilians. Hamas's October 7 attack targeted a music festival, kibbutz homes, and families — executing children, raping women, burning people alive — with no legal or moral justification under any framework. Common Article 3 of the Geneva Conventions explicitly prohibits violence against civilians, hostage-taking, and outrages upon personal dignity. Firing unguided rockets into civilian centers — over 20,000 since 2001 — is a war crime regardless of claimed political purpose. When a negotiated state is repeatedly offered and refused, the "liberation" framing loses credibility.

Round 1 · Pro-Palestine

International law explicitly recognizes the right of peoples under occupation to resist — including by armed means. UN GA Resolutions 3314 (1974) and 37/43 (1982) reaffirm "the legitimacy of the struggle of peoples for liberation from colonial and foreign domination and foreign occupation by all available means, including armed struggle." The same framework that justified the French Resistance and South African armed resistance against apartheid applies to Palestinians. Critical distinction: targeting military forces — protected; targeting civilians — a war crime. When non-violent resistance is met with live ammunition (2018 Great March: 200+ unarmed protesters killed by snipers), demanding Palestinians restrict to non-violence demands acceptance of subjugation.

Round 2 · Pro-Israel Rebuttal

UNGA Resolution 37/43 is non-binding — it carries no force of international law. The French Resistance and ANC did not systematically murder children at music festivals, take civilian hostages including infants, or rape women as deliberate war strategy. Conflating those movements with Hamas is historically illiterate. The 2018 Gaza protests were not uniformly unarmed — the IDF documented armed infiltrators and IED placements within protest cover. The right to resist does not extend to deliberate targeting of non-combatants under any credible reading of international humanitarian law, including Common Article 3.

Round 2 · Pro-Palestine Rebuttal

October 7 atrocities were war crimes — this debate does not require defending them. But the opponent uses Hamas's crimes to retroactively delegitimize Palestinian civilian rights, which is a logical fallacy. Common Article 3 applies equally to Israel's conduct in Gaza — indiscriminate bombing of refugee camps, hospitals, and bakeries also constitutes war crimes. Camp David 2000 offered Palestinian cantons disconnected by Israeli roads with Israel retaining sovereignty over borders, airspace, and electromagnetic spectrum — not a viable state. Dennis Ross's own maps confirm this. No party is obligated to accept an offer that falls short of their legal rights.

Q5

Has the international community been fair and balanced in its approach to the conflict?

Round 1 · Pro-Israel

The international community has been profoundly unfair to Israel. The UNHRC passed 78 resolutions condemning Israel between 2006 and 2022, compared to 7 for Syria, 6 for North Korea, and 4 for Iran. The UN General Assembly's Agenda Item 7 singles out Israel as the only country with a permanent standing condemnation item. The 1975 UN resolution equating Zionism with racism (revoked 1991) reveals how political manipulation has driven international positions. Robert Bernstein, founder of Human Rights Watch, resigned in protest over his own organization's disproportionate focus on Israel. The double standard is documented, not a talking point.

Round 1 · Pro-Palestine

The US has vetoed over 45 UN Security Council resolutions critical of Israel since 1972. In February 2024, the US vetoed a ceasefire resolution while Gaza's death toll surpassed 30,000. Oslo required Palestinians to recognize Israel's right to exist, but Israel was never required to recognize Palestinian statehood or halt settlement expansion — which accelerated after Oslo. The EU declares settlements "illegal" but imposes no penalties. The ICC's 2021 investigation was met with US threats of sanctions. Israel receives $3.8 billion annually in US military aid with no human rights conditions. This is the opposite of balance.

Round 2 · Pro-Israel Rebuttal

US vetoes reflect geopolitical alliance, not evidence of Palestinian righteousness. The UNHRC's institutional bias is documented — it included members like Libya and Venezuela while uniquely targeting Israel. The Oslo asymmetry reflected reality: Israel was recognizing a non-state actor that had previously committed mass terrorism — a massive concession. Military aid comes with end-use restrictions under the Arms Export Control Act. Imperfect accountability is not proof of impunity.

Round 2 · Pro-Palestine Rebuttal

The disproportionate UNHRC focus reflects 57 years of occupation generating visible, reviewable, daily violations. Syria and North Korea obstruct UN investigators entirely — the comparison is invalid. Robert Bernstein's 2009 op-ed argued accountability should apply less to open societies — a perverse inversion of democratic accountability. Criticizing Israeli state policy is not antisemitism — conflating the two suppresses legitimate human rights discourse.

Q6

Is the two-state solution still viable, and which side bears more responsibility for its failure?

Round 1 · Pro-Israel

The two-state solution remains the only viable framework, and primary responsibility for its failure lies with Palestinian leadership's repeated rejection of credible offers. In 1937 (Peel Commission) — Arab leadership rejected. In 1947 (UN Partition Plan) — Arabs launched war. In 2000 (Camp David) — rejected. In 2008 (Olmert) — 93.5% of the West Bank plus land swaps, Palestinian capital in East Jerusalem — Abbas did not respond. Hamas's founding charter calls for the destruction of Israel and the killing of Jews. A two-state solution requires both parties to accept the other's existence. Israeli settlement expansion complicates the path and deserves criticism, but negotiated land swaps have been proposed repeatedly.

Round 1 · Pro-Palestine

The two-state solution has been rendered nearly non-viable by deliberate Israeli policy. Over 700,000 settlers now live in the West Bank and East Jerusalem, strategically placed to fragment Palestinian territory into non-contiguous bantustans, severing East Jerusalem from any future Palestinian capital. The settlement enterprise has accelerated under every Israeli government regardless of party. Camp David did not offer a viable Palestinian state — the offer required Israeli sovereignty over large settlement blocs, no Palestinian control of Jerusalem's holy sites, and no resolution of the refugee question. The Palestinian Authority has recognized Israel's right to exist since 1993 — a recognition Israel has never reciprocated regarding Palestinian statehood.

Round 2 · Pro-Israel Rebuttal

Gaza has zero Israeli settlers yet produced October 7 — this challenges the premise that settlements are the sole obstacle. Camp David included 91-94% of the West Bank, a Palestinian capital in East Jerusalem, and control of the Temple Mount's surface. Robert Malley himself later acknowledged Palestinian responsibility for Camp David's failure. Hamas emerged from the Muslim Brotherhood's ideological project — radicalization is a choice, not an inevitability of occupation.

Round 2 · Pro-Palestine Rebuttal

Clayton Swisher's "The Truth About Camp David" and Robert Malley's own Foreign Affairs article documented that no formal final written offer was ever tabled at Camp David. Clinton said Arafat made mistakes but never asserted a complete offer was rejected. The 2017 Hamas charter revision — accepting a Palestinian state on 1967 borders — was entirely ignored by the opponent. The Arab Peace Initiative of 2002 offered 22 Arab states' full normalization for 1967 borders — Israel rejected it without a counter-offer. Actions speak: settlement construction never stopped during any negotiation.

Q7

How should the Palestinian refugee right of return be addressed?

Round 1 · Pro-Israel

The right of return must be addressed through compensation, resettlement in a future Palestinian state, and negotiated family reunification — not a blanket return that would demographically dismantle the world's only Jewish state. UNRWA's definition uniquely passes refugee status down through generations indefinitely, inflating numbers from roughly 700,000 original 1948 refugees to over 5 million today. Approximately 800,000 to 1 million Jewish refugees were expelled from Arab countries after 1948 and absorbed with no UN agency and no calls for return. A just resolution must be symmetrical.

Round 1 · Pro-Palestine

The Palestinian right of return is not a political demand — it is an individual legal right. UN General Assembly Resolution 194 (1948) affirmed that "refugees wishing to return to their homes and live at peace with their neighbours should be permitted to do so at the earliest practicable date" — reaffirmed over 130 times. Approximately 5.9 million Palestinian refugees are registered with UNRWA. Their villages — over 400 destroyed — are documented by Israeli historian Benny Morris. This was organized expulsion, including documented massacres such as Deir Yassin. The argument that Palestinian rights cannot be honored because they would alter Israel's demographic majority explicitly privileges ethnic dominance over legal rights.

Round 2 · Pro-Israel Rebuttal

Resolution 194 has conditionality — "wishing to return and live in peace with their neighbours." Benny Morris — cited by the opponent — concluded no master plan for expulsion existed and that some measures were historically understandable in the context of a war of annihilation launched against Israel. The 800,000 Jewish refugees expelled from Arab countries receive zero international advocacy. UNRWA's multigenerational definition inflates numbers from 700,000 to 5.9 million — a figure that would demographically eliminate the Jewish state by design.

Round 2 · Pro-Palestine Rebuttal

UNRWA's multigenerational definition exists because the refugees were never resettled — you cannot extinguish a refugee crisis by redefining the victims. The 1951 Refugee Convention recognizes inherited refugee status when original displacement remains unresolved. Regarding Jewish refugees from Arab countries: Israel used those refugees as citizens and built a state around them, while simultaneously arguing Palestinian refugees have no right of return to the state built on their homes. Resolution 194, reaffirmed over 130 times, establishes the right of return as individual and inalienable — not subject to demographic calculations by the dispossessing party.

Q8

Are Israeli settlements in the West Bank a legitimate expression of sovereignty or a violation of international law?

Round 1 · Pro-Israel

The legal picture is more contested than critics acknowledge. The West Bank's legal status derives from 1949 armistice lines — not recognized international borders. Jordan, which controlled the West Bank from 1948 to 1967, had no legitimate sovereign claim — it had seized the territory in an offensive war. When Israel captured the West Bank in 1967 in a defensive war, it entered territory with no recognized sovereign. Article 49 of the Fourth Geneva Convention was written specifically in response to Nazi Germany's deportation of populations — voluntary civilian movement into disputed territory is legally distinguishable. Former State Department Legal Advisor Stephen Schwebel and Judge Eugene Rostow argued Israel has valid claims pending a final peace agreement.

Round 1 · Pro-Palestine

Israeli settlements are unambiguously illegal under international law. Article 49 of the Fourth Geneva Convention is explicit: "The Occupying Power shall not deport or transfer parts of its own civilian population into the territory it occupies." The ICRC — authoritative interpreter of Geneva Conventions — has repeatedly confirmed this applies to Israeli settlements. The ICJ's 2004 opinion stated settlements "have been established in breach of international law." UNSC Resolution 2334 (2016) declared settlements have "no legal validity" and constitute "a flagrant violation under international law." Over 700,000 settlers now occupy over 42% of West Bank land. Water resources are diverted at ratios of 6:1. Settlement infrastructure physically fragments Palestinian territory.

Round 2 · Pro-Israel Rebuttal

The ICJ's 2004 advisory opinion was non-binding. UNSC 2334 passed because the Obama administration deliberately abstained — a parting political shot, not a legal transformation of the territories' status. The West Bank's legal status remains "disputed," not "occupied sovereign Palestinian territory," because no Palestinian state has ever exercised sovereignty there. Jordan's 1948-1967 control was itself unrecognized by most states. The Israeli Supreme Court has ordered route modifications and removed illegal outposts, demonstrating functioning judicial oversight.

Round 2 · Pro-Palestine Rebuttal

Article 49(6) applies regardless of the prior sovereignty question — confirmed explicitly by the ICJ in both 2004 and 2024. The Schwebel-Rostow minority view was rejected by the UN Security Council in Resolutions 446, 452, and 465 — all passed with US support. The French text of Resolution 242 uses "des territoires" implying all territories. Over 700,000 settlers now live in the West Bank while Israel claims the matter is "under negotiation" — this is running out the clock, not preparation for a negotiated solution.

Q9

What role does each side's governance play in perpetuating or resolving the conflict?

Round 1 · Pro-Israel

Israel is a functioning democracy with independent courts, a free press, an active civil society, and civilian oversight of the military. Its Supreme Court regularly rules against the government on settlement issues. Israel has made peace with Egypt (1979) and Jordan (1994), proving its government can deliver durable agreements. Palestinian governance presents a troubling picture: the PA has governed without elections since 2006, the "pay-to-slay" policy (Law No. 14 of 2004) provides salaries to Palestinians convicted of terrorism, spending hundreds of millions annually. In Gaza, Hamas has suppressed opposition, executed rivals, and embedded military infrastructure in hospitals, schools, and mosques.

Round 1 · Pro-Palestine

Palestinian governance is deeply flawed — Hamas's 2023 attacks were war crimes, the PA is corrupt and undemocratic. But these failures must be contextualized against structural conditions. The PA was created by Oslo as a subcontractor of Israeli occupation — denied control of borders, airspace, water, and electromagnetic spectrum. When Hamas won the 2006 Palestinian legislative elections — declared free and fair by the Carter Center — Israel, the US, and EU immediately imposed an economic siege, signaling democratic outcomes are only accepted when producing compliant leadership. Benjamin Netanyahu has openly stated he blocked the two-state solution as a political objective. Palestinian governance failures occur under occupation; Israeli governance failures are the occupation.

Round 2 · Pro-Israel Rebuttal

Yasser Arafat diverted hundreds of millions in international aid into personal accounts and factional patronage — documented by the IMF and World Bank. Hamas won the 2006 election and then violently seized Gaza in 2007, throwing Fatah members off rooftops — the Carter Center certified the vote, not the subsequent coup. The Israeli Supreme Court has ruled against the government on settlements and barrier route issues more times than acknowledged. Blaming occupation for every internal Palestinian governance failure denies Palestinian agency.

Round 2 · Pro-Palestine Rebuttal

The "pay-to-slay" characterization misrepresents a social welfare system for imprisoned family members — analogous to how many states support families of imprisoned individuals. Palestinian elections were not held partly because Israel arrested elected Hamas legislators and the US refused to recognize results — the democratic process was sabotaged externally. Egypt 1979 and Jordan 1994 are irrelevant comparisons: those states made peace over their own sovereign territories. Israel has never offered Palestinians what it offered Egypt — full sovereignty, recognized borders, and end of occupation. The comparison proves the point: when Israel is asked to give the same terms to Palestinians, it refuses.

Q10

What would a just and lasting peace look like, and which side's vision comes closer to achieving it?

Round 1 · Pro-Israel

A just and lasting peace requires two states for two peoples, mutual recognition, security guarantees, negotiated borders with land swaps, compensation for refugees resettled in Palestine or third countries, and a shared arrangement for Jerusalem. Rabin was assassinated for pursuing Oslo. Barak offered a state. Olmert offered the most generous package in history. The Abraham Accords of 2020 demonstrate regional peace is achievable when partners are willing. Hamas's vision — enshrined in its founding documents — is a Muslim state from the river to the sea with no Jewish sovereignty. This is not a negotiating position; it is an eliminationist program. Israel's vision is the only one containing the structural possibility of coexistence.

Round 1 · Pro-Palestine

A just peace must be built on international law, equality, and human rights — not on the current power imbalance. It requires full end of the military occupation, recognition of Palestinian self-determination and statehood, a just resolution of the refugee question per Resolution 194, and equal rights for all people living between the river and the sea. The Arab Peace Initiative of 2002, endorsed by all 22 Arab League states, offered Israel full normalization with the entire Arab and Muslim world in exchange for withdrawal to 1967 borders and a just refugee settlement. Israel never formally responded. The Palestinian vision is rooted in international law and UN resolutions. The status quo — permanent occupation, expanding settlements — offers neither justice nor lasting peace.

Round 2 · Pro-Israel Rebuttal

The Arab Peace Initiative was not rejected outright — Israel engaged it and identified fundamental problems, including the right of return clause that would demographically end Israel as a Jewish state. The one-state solution with "equal rights" is a formula that in practice ends Jewish self-determination — the one form of national self-determination the opponent apparently considers illegitimate. The initiative arrived during the height of the Second Intifada. A negotiated two-state solution remains the only framework that honors both peoples' claims.

Round 2 · Pro-Palestine Rebuttal

The Clinton Parameters were never formally accepted by Israel — Barak's government was collapsing and no written Israeli acceptance was deposited. Even those parameters left Israel controlling Palestinian borders, airspace, and requiring military presence in the Jordan Valley — not full sovereignty. The "river to sea" phrase the opponent uses to condemn Palestinians is simultaneously used by Israeli coalition ministers — Finance Minister Smotrich and National Security Minister Ben-Gvir openly call for annexation and ethnic cleansing. If eliminationist rhetoric disqualifies a side, address Israeli ministers first. 57 years of settlement expansion during every "negotiation" demonstrates which party forecloses the two-state solution on the ground.
